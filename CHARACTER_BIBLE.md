# BWN Kids — Character Bible

Paste-ready descriptions for building **consistent** comic characters in Dashtoon (or any
character-reference tool). Workflow: for each character, generate a **reference sheet once**
(front view, neutral pose, plain background), save it to the tool's character library, then
reuse it in every panel. Always include the **lock phrase** verbatim so the face stays the same.

## House style (use on every generation)
> Warm, friendly children's-comic style. Clean bold outlines, bright flat colours, soft
> shading. Dignified and real — never caricature. Vertical webtoon panels (mobile scroll).
> Kind faces, expressive eyes. Ages 3–13 audience.

Tone rules (from our editorial guide): belonging not difference; brave, beautiful, beautifully
made; real history told simply; no scary or violent imagery; no buzzwords.

---

## 1. Kojo — boy guide (original character)
**Lock phrase:** `Kojo, a cheerful 9-year-old Black boy, short rounded afro, round face, big bright eyes, gap-toothed smile, mustard-yellow t-shirt with a small black star, green shorts, white trainers`
A curious, brave kid who time-travels through history with Ama. Warm, asks lots of questions.

## 2. Ama — girl guide (original character)
**Lock phrase:** `Ama, a confident 9-year-old Black girl, two afro puffs with red beads, round face, big bright eyes, warm smile, teal dress with a small black star, orange leggings, white trainers`
Kojo's friend. Clever, kind, the one who explains things. Equal partner — girls lead too.

> Kojo & Ama are the throughline: every comic follows them meeting a real figure or visiting a
> real place. They keep the series consistent and give young readers someone to ride along with.

---

## 3. Marcus Garvey 🇯🇲
**Lock phrase:** `Marcus Garvey, Black Jamaican leader, 1920s, stocky build, round kind face, short hair, smart dark double-breasted suit, tall plumed ceremonial hat, confident warm expression`
*Who he is (kid-level):* A man from Jamaica who told every Black child to stand tall and be
proud. The black star on our logo is his idea. Catchphrase to weave in: "Up, you mighty people."

## 4. Toussaint Louverture 🇭🇹
**Lock phrase:** `Toussaint Louverture, Black Haitian leader, 1790s, slim, thoughtful brave face, short hair, blue and gold military general's coat with gold buttons, bicorne hat, wise calm expression`
*Who he is:* A brave, clever leader who helped make Haiti the first country where free Black
people ruled themselves. Theme: being brave can change everything.

## 5. Yaa Asantewaa 🇬🇭
**Lock phrase:** `Yaa Asantewaa, Black Ashanti queen mother from Ghana, regal upright posture, strong proud kind face, colourful kente cloth wrap, gold jewellery and gold headband, dignified expression`
*Who she is:* A queen mother in Ghana who was brave for her people and protected the Golden
Stool. Theme: girls can be strong leaders too. Catchphrase: "If the men will not go forward,
then we women will."

## Quick start in Dashtoon
1. New project → vertical / webtoon.
2. For each character above: **Create character → paste the lock phrase → generate → pick the
   best → save to character library.** (Do Kojo & Ama first — they're in every strip.)
3. Write a short script (panel = one beat). Use script-to-comic, dropping characters in by name.
4. Keep panels simple: 1 idea per panel, big friendly speech bubbles, few words.
5. Export → publish behind the Comics door (`comics.html`).

## First comic idea (to test the pipeline)
**"Kojo & Ama Meet Marcus Garvey."** 6 panels: the two kids find a glowing black star → it lifts
them to 1920s Jamaica → they meet Garvey → he tells them "you are somebody, stand tall" → they
see the Black Star Line ships → back home, prouder. Tests two locked guides + one figure in one
short strip. If consistency holds across those 6 panels, the workflow works.
